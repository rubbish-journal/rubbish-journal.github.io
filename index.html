About the Journal

"In a world obsessed with impact factors and h-indices, RUBBISH dares to ask: what about everything else?"

Aims & Scope

RUBBISH: A Journal of Abandoned Scholarly Endeavours is a peer-reviewed*, open-access academic journal dedicated to the publication of work that the mainstream scholarly ecosystem has deemed unworthy, unfinished, or simply unfortunate.

We welcome submissions across all disciplines, including but not limited to: rejected manuscripts (the more rejection letters, the better), failed experiments and null results, half-written dissertations, conference papers that were never presented because the author overslept, literature reviews that went nowhere, and strongly worded responses to Reviewer 2 that were never sent.

RUBBISH operates on the principle that the academic dustbin is full of knowledge waiting to be rediscovered. We believe that failure is not the opposite of contribution‚Äîit is its most honest form.

*Peer review at RUBBISH consists of at least one person reading the abstract and saying "yeah, that tracks."

Publication Ethics

RUBBISH adheres to the highest standards of publication ethics, which is to say we have standards, and they are technically non-zero. We follow COPE guidelines to the extent that we have read the summary. Plagiarism is unacceptable; however, self-plagiarism from your own abandoned drafts is not only acceptable but encouraged. We do not fabricate data, although we acknowledge that some of our published data may have been fabricated by the original authors before they gave up on the project entirely.

Submit Your Manuscript

RUBBISH welcomes submissions from scholars at all career stages, from undergraduate students who just discovered their entire thesis was based on a misunderstanding, to emeritus professors who finally want to publish that thing they've been sitting on since 1987.

Submission Categories

Research Articles ‚Äî Original research that didn't quite work out. Maximum 10,000 words, minimum effort acceptable.

Failed Experiments ‚Äî Detailed accounts of methodological disasters. The more catastrophic, the better.

Rejected Manuscripts ‚Äî Papers rejected elsewhere. Please include original rejection letters for our amusement and your catharsis.

Short Communications ‚Äî Brief reports of academic misadventures, including but not limited to: accidentally deleting datasets, presenting at the wrong conference, and discovering your control group was actually the treatment group.

Letters to Reviewer 2 ‚Äî A dedicated space for correspondence you always wanted to send but never did.

Formatting Guidelines

We accept manuscripts in any format, including but not limited to: PDF, Word, LaTeX, napkin scans, voice memos transcribed by a sleep-deprived PhD student, and PowerPoint slides from a talk you gave in 2019 that "could totally be a paper." We recommend Times New Roman 12pt because we are traditionalists, but we won't reject Comic Sans‚Äîwe don't reject anything.

Review Process

All submissions undergo a rigorous review process consisting of: (1) an editor reads the title, (2) the editor checks that the PDF opens, and (3) the editor confirms the author has not accidentally submitted their grocery list (although, if sufficiently annotated, grocery lists may be considered under our Short Communications category). Typical turnaround time is 24 hours, because life is too short to wait 9 months for a desk rejection.
